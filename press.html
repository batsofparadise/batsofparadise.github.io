Toronto duo Bats of Paradise release their debut EP June 16, channeling the drive of 90s guitar rock, the catharsis of post-rock, and the swirl of shoegaze. The lead single, Head Like a Hornet’s Nest, is available everywhere June 2:

ABOUT THE ALBUM

Bats of Paradise was born in a darkened lab at a neuroscience research institute north of Toronto. Brought together by a grad school research project after years apart, Phil Johnston and Mark Schlosser rediscovered their shared reverence for the guitar-driven music they had bonded over as teenagers. Writing began that night – the first of many nights spent in a rented garage refining their dynamic, emotive approach to songwriting. Facing the challenges of long-distance collaboration over the pandemic, they set themselves some simple rules to capture the momentum of those original sessions on their first EP: no synths or electronics, no involved sound design, just guitars and a drum kit. Over the course of these six songs, Bats of Paradise steady themselves against the vertigo of life in the 2020s by embracing its contradictions: the urgent and the abstract, the noisy and serene.
